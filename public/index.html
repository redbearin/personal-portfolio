About Me

My interest in programing began when I was part of a team that designed and built a laboratory robot for a Swiss company.

I became more involved in the field when I worked for a medical device start-up that developed an electronic control system for an orthopedic appliance. The microprocessor in this device was controlled by software written in machine language. In addition to managing engineering projects and writing the technical parts of the patent application and the Food and Drug Administration 510(K) submission, I also handled marketing for the company.

I also spent a number of years working in product development, business, marketing, and education. Again, I worked on teams that included software developers, engineers, and designers.

When I am not reading or writing about leadership, team dynamics, coding, or biological computing, I like visiting modern art galleries and museums.
