About Me

I am a web developer who enjoys leading teams.

My interest in programming began when I was in college. My final project team designed and built a laboratory robot. While I was responsible for mechanical design elements, it was interesting to see the intersection between mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, and software development.

Next I worked with a medical device start-up. Again, I was exposed to software engineering. Even though I was mostly involved in project management, it was interesting to see first hand how assembly language programming could be used in a real world application.

After this assignment, I spent a number of years working in business, marketing, and product development. While I was not programming myself, I led teams that included software engineers and designers.

Recently, I decided to learn how to code. It has been a fun process. I love the creative element and the challenge of writing code that meets specific design and technical requirements. I also enjoy the team aspect.

When I am not reading or writing about leadership, team dynamics, or coding, I like visiting modern art galleries and museums and reading about biological computing.
